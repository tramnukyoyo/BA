## HYDROGEN SUPPLY CHAIN

**Production:** 96% from fossil fuels (48% natural gas, 30% oil, 18% coal, only 4% electrolysis)
- **Steam Methane Reforming (SMR)** dominates (75%+ hydrogen, but 750¬∞C+ temperature, low efficiency)
- Most economical but not green

**Storage:** Compressed gas standard (35-950 bar depending on application)
- **Cascaded system:** 3-stage pressure optimization (reduces cooling energy)
- Pre-cooling to -40¬∞C for 700 bar dispensing

**Transportation:**
- **Short range (<270 km):** Tube trailers (200-500 bar)
- **Long range (270-1000 km):** Liquid tankers or pipelines
- Pipeline most efficient for long distances

**Refueling:** 700 bar (cars) / 350 bar (buses)

## KOREA'S ROLLOUT STRATEGY (Model for Others)

### Production Approach
- **Primary:** Hydrogen as by-product from LNG facilities (cheap, no extra investment)
- **Secondary:** Import liquefied hydrogen
- **Future:** Local electrolysis expansion

### Supply Chain Problem
- Most H‚ÇÇ transported via pipelines
- **Result:** Uneven geographic distribution across country
- **Solution:** Deploy local extractors + electrolysis in provincial plants

### Government Support
- Subsidies for private HRS installation
- **Target price:** 2,500 won/kg by 2050 (currently much higher)
- Combined 2019 Roadmap + 2022 Basic Plan

### Infrastructure Timeline
- Phased HRS deployment aligned with FCEV targets
- Solve "chicken-and-egg" by government-led coordination
