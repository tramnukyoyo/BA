## Phase 2: Thesis Research Foundation

**Trigger:** Exposé approved by professor
**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Goal:** Comprehensive literature review + detailed thesis outline

**Requirements Covered:**
- FOUND-01, FOUND-02, FOUND-03, FOUND-04, FOUND-05

### Key Activities

**Weeks 1-2: Deep Literature Review**
- Systematic review of 40+ sources (expand from current 12)
- Organize by research theme:
  - Regulatory frameworks (ISO, EU, US, China, emerging)
  - Technical infrastructure (storage, compression, pipelines, distribution)
  - Logistics & transport modes (pipeline, truck, rail, ship)
  - Economic models (capex, opex, station costs)
  - Implementation strategies (policy, PPPs, pilot lessons)
- Extract key quotes and findings per source
- Map sources to chapter sections

**Weeks 3-4: Create Detailed Outline**
- Chapter-by-chapter breakdown with section-level arguments
- Visual frameworks: concept maps, comparison tables, decision matrices
- Detailed outline for each chapter:
  - Chapter 1: Introduction (4-6 pages)
  - Chapter 2: Literature Review (12-15 pages)
  - Chapter 3: Regulatory Analysis (8-10 pages)
  - Chapter 4: Logistics & Technical (10-12 pages)
  - Chapter 5: Implementation Strategies (8-10 pages)

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] 40+ sources organized by category
- [ ] Full bibliography expanded (Harvard format)
- [ ] Detailed thesis structure with section-level outlines
- [ ] Visual frameworks (concept maps, comparison tables)

**Success Criteria:**
- [ ] Coverage of all 5 research sub-questions mapped to sections

## Phase 3: Core Content Development

**Trigger:** Phase 2 complete + outline approved
**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Goal:** Write Chapters 1-4 (40-45 pages of substantive content)

**Requirements Covered:**
- INTRO-01 through INTRO-05
- LIT-01 through LIT-06
- REG-01 through REG-06
- TECH-01 through TECH-07

### Chapter Breakdown

#### Chapter 1: Einführung (Introduction) — 4-6 pages
- Energy transition context & hydrogen's role
- Problem statement: infrastructure gap (chicken-and-egg)
- 3 primary research questions + 5 sub-questions
- Thesis structure preview
- Scope boundaries

**Success Criteria:**
- Problem framing is compelling and specific
- RQs are testable and hierarchical
- Reader understands why this research matters
- Scope is explicit (what IS/ISN'T included)

#### Chapter 2: Literaturübersicht (Literature Review) — 12-15 pages
- Hydrogen role in energy transition (global context)
- Current infrastructure status globally
- Existing research on barriers:
  - Regulatory (standards, certifications, cross-border)
  - Technical (storage, compression, pipeline maturity)
  - Logistical (transport modes, distribution, costs)
  - Economic (capex/opex, ROI, financing)
  - Implementation (policy, coordination, pilot lessons)
- Positioning of thesis contribution
- Clear identification of research gaps

**Success Criteria:**
- Comprehensive coverage (40+ sources)
- Clear thematic organization
- Specific research gaps identified
- Academic tone consistent throughout

#### Chapter 3: Regulatorische Herausforderungen (Regulatory Barriers) — 8-10 pages
- ISO standards and harmonization efforts
- Regional regulatory comparison (EU, US, China, emerging economies)
- Safety & certification frameworks
- Cross-border transmission regulation
- Opportunities for regulatory acceleration

**Success Criteria:**
- Specific regulatory frameworks documented
- Comparative analysis clear
- Evidence-based assessment with examples
- Feasible solutions proposed

#### Chapter 4: Logistische & Technische Infrastruktur (Logistics & Technical) — 10-12 pages
- Production-to-distribution chain components
- Transport modes: pipeline, truck, rail, ship (costs & feasibility)
- Storage technologies & economics
- Compression, purity, and safety challenges
- Refueling station design

**Success Criteria:**
- Technical feasibility clearly explained
- Cost breakdowns detailed (capex/opex)
- Real-world examples provided
- Realistic timeline assessment

### Writing Schedule
- **Weeks 1-2:** Chapter 1 + Chapter 2 (first half)
- **Weeks 3-4:** Chapter 2 (second half) + Chapter 3
- **Weeks 5-6:** Chapter 4 + internal review

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] `thesis/chapter-01-intro.tex` (4-6 pages)
- [ ] `thesis/chapter-02-litreview.tex` (12-15 pages)
- [ ] `thesis/chapter-03-regulatory.tex` (8-10 pages)
- [ ] `thesis/chapter-04-infrastructure.tex` (10-12 pages)
- [ ] All sources properly cited (Harvard format)
- [ ] Academic German register consistent

**Gate:** Phase 3 Complete when all 4 chapters meet quality criteria
