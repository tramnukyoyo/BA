## 3. SECTION-BY-SECTION CONTENT PATTERNS

### Section 1: Ausgangslage (Initial Situation/Context)

**Purpose:** Hook reader, establish relevance, define key concepts

**Structure:**
1. Open with a provocative/contextual statement
   - Example: "In dem Zeitalter der Digitalisierung sind die Begriffe..."
2. Define key terms (2-3 main concepts)
   - Simple definitions with citations
3. Justify why this matters (market data, trends)
   - Include statistics/figures with proper citations
   - Can reference charts/graphs
4. Bridge to problem statement

**Key Characteristics:**
- Active voice, present tense
- Approximately 400-600 words
- Includes at least 1 visual (chart, figure)
- 2-3 citations for definitions and statistics
- Transitions smoothly to problem statement

---

### Section 2: Problemstellung / Herausforderung und Forschungsstand

**Purpose:** Define the research gap, establish what is NOT known

**Structure:**
1. Identify the specific challenge/barrier
   - Example: "Die Integration von KI-Technologien kann für Unternehmen äußerst kompliziert ausfallen..."
2. Provide evidence (statistics on adoption, maturity levels)
3. Describe current state of knowledge (research landscape)
   - What is known? What are experts saying?
   - Can cite studies with sample sizes, findings
4. Highlight the gap
   - What remains unclear or unstudied?
5. End with clear problem statement

**Key Characteristics:**
- Uses concrete statistics (% adoption rates, study sample sizes)
- References multiple sources
- Identifies maturity levels or stages in the field
- Shows what has been explored vs. what hasn't
- Approximately 400-500 words

---

### Section 3: Formulierung der Forschungsfragen

**Purpose:** Pose clear, researchable questions

**Format:**
- Bullet points (using ➢ symbol)
- 2-3 research questions
- Questions should be:
  - Open-ended (not yes/no)
  - Specific and measurable
  - Connected to the problem statement
  - Hierarchical (one main, subordinate questions possible)

**Example Structure:**
```
➢ [Primary question about impact/influence on X, Y, Z variables]
➢ [Secondary question about specific application/mechanism]
➢ [Tertiary question about differentiation/comparison]
```

---

### Section 4: Zielsetzung und Aufgabenstellung (Objectives & Tasks)

**Purpose:** Explain what thesis will accomplish and how

**Structure:**
1. Restate introduction/relevance briefly
2. Outline the learning pathway for the reader
3. Describe each major chapter's purpose (without numbering chapters yet)
   - Example: "werden im Rahmen dieser Abschlussarbeit die Grundlagen der wesentlichen Begriffe... erläutert"
4. Show how sections connect to research questions
5. State the final deliverable/knowledge outcome

**Key Characteristics:**
- Uses "werden...erläutert" (will be explained) structure
- Chronological flow through thesis narrative
- Explains the pedagogical value for the reader
- Connects theory → application → discussion
- 400-600 words

---

### Section 5: Gliederung (Detailed Outline of Thesis)

**Purpose:** Preview the complete thesis structure

**Format:**
Hierarchical numbering (up to 3-4 levels):
```
1) Chapter Title
   1.1 Subsection
   1.2 Subsection
      1.2.1 Sub-subsection
2) Next Chapter
   2.1 Subsection
   etc.
```

**Typical Chapter Structure (6 chapters):**

| Chapter | Purpose | Typical Subsections |
|---------|---------|------------------|
| 1 | Introduction | Motivation, Structure, Methodology |
| 2 | Theoretical Foundations | 3 major theoretical topics, each with sub-components |
| 3 | Applications/State of Art | 4-5 specific application areas |
| 4 | Effects & Potentials | 4-5 impact dimensions (efficiency, flexibility, sustainability, etc.) |
| 5 | Discussion/Analysis | Synthesis and evaluation of technologies/findings |
| 6 | Summary & Outlook | Conclusions and future research |

**Content Notes:**
- Should have 15-25 subsections total
- Balanced distribution across chapters
- Each subsection should be researchable
- Avoid overly deep nesting (max 3-4 levels)

### Section 6: Literaturverzeichnis (Bibliography)

**Citation Style:** Harvard (Author-Date)

**Format Rules:**
- Alphabetical by author surname
- Multiple works by same author: chronological
- All metadata included: author, year, title, source, URL/DOI
- Proper formatting with italics, full titles

**Example Entry Types Present:**

## 4. STYLISTIC & LINGUISTIC PATTERNS

### Language Register
- **Formal academic German** (Hochdeutsch)
- Active voice strongly preferred (e.g., "werden...erläutert" not passive)
- Present and future tenses for descriptions
- Past tense for citations/established findings
- No colloquialisms or contractions

### Sentence Structure
- Longer, complex sentences
- Proper use of subordinate clauses
- Clear logical connectors (Daher, Aufgrund dessen, Dadurch, etc.)
- Technical terminology defined on first use

### Citation Integration
- Superscript footnotes for sources (e.g., ^1)
- Or in-text (Author Year) format combined with footnotes
- Every statistic, definition, concept must be cited
- Primary sources preferred for definitions

### Formatting Conventions
- Section headings: **Bold**, consistent font size
- Subsection headings: **Bold**, smaller
- Figures: Numbered (Abbildung 1, etc.) with captions below
- Tables: Numbered with captions
- Lists: Bullet points (➢) or numbered (1), (2)
- Citations: Superscript numbers or author-date in text

## 6. KEY QUALITY INDICATORS

✅ **Strong Exposés Include:**
- Clear narrative arc from context → problem → solution pathway
- Specific, measurable research questions
- Comprehensive literature engagement (8+ sources)
- Logical thesis structure that directly addresses questions
- Professional formatting and typography
- Visual aids (figures/concept map) supporting arguments
- Harvard or consistent citation style
- Appropriate length (7-8 pages)
- Active, confident academic voice

❌ **Weaknesses to Avoid:**
- Vague or yes/no research questions
- Outline that doesn't connect to research questions
- Missing concept map or visual summary
- Sparse citations (< 5 sources)
- Unclear connection between chapters
- Passive voice throughout
- Inconsistent formatting or citation style
